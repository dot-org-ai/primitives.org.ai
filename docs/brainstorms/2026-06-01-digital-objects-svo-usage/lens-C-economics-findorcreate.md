# Lens C — Economics / market-design / the findOrCreate gate at 1M-startup scale

## Lens thesis

At 1,000,000 startups the cascade is not generating a million isolated documents; it is **growing one deduplicated mega-graph** where the marginal cost of the Nth startup approaches the cost of *linking* the handful of genuinely-novel entities it introduces. Every `->`/`~>` edge a cascade is about to mint is an economic decision: pay LLM tokens to *compose* a new tail, or pay a cheap embedding query to *link* an existing canonical one. The SVO layer is the instrument that makes that decision routable and priceable. `Frame` carries the per-relation typing (`fan`, direction) the gate reads to pick functional-vs-fuzzy regime; `TokenStratum` declares, per field, whether content is an identity key (never regenerate), a fill-once slot, a bandit-optimizable variant, or cheap throwaway prose; the two embeddings modes decide whether two strings are *the same thing* (COLLAPSE) or *a good answer to a query* (MATCH). Spent well, these three knobs turn the cost curve from `O(startups × edges × LLM)` into `O(novel-entities × LLM) + O(all-edges × embedding)` — and the second term is rounding error. *Embeddings discriminate; LLMs compose* — and the entire savings live in discriminating before composing.

## Use cases

### 1. The `producedBy → Industry` functional-regime link
**Use case.** Startup #412,008 ("a Shopify app that reconciles 3PL invoices") cascades to a `producedBy → Industry` edge. The cascade calls `$.Industry.findOrCreate("3rd-party logistics software")` framed as a `~>` relationship resolution. The Frame declares this relation `fan<3, direction-bearing`. The gate runs the per-relation InfoNCE adapter rank (not raw cosine), gets H@1 0.92 on the canonical `Logistics Software` Industry Thing, and returns `{kind:'link'}`. No LLM token is spent; the edge points at an entity 40,000 prior startups already share.
**Economic mechanism.** Functional regime → asymmetric MATCH embedding + InfoNCE rank → high-precision link at ~$0 marginal LLM. The alternative (mint) would have spent a composition call and fractured the Industry vocabulary into 40,000 near-synonyms, destroying every downstream rollup query.
**Demand on the interface.** `Frame` must carry per-relation `fan` and `direction` so the gate can *read off the Verb's frame* which regime to enter — the gate must not have to guess functional-vs-fuzzy from runtime data. `PredicateSpec.{rel,dstType,fan,direction}` must be derivable from the `Frame` of the Verb that owns the relation.

### 2. `suppliesTo` fuzzy-regime ratify pass
**Use case.** A B2B-marketplace startup mints `Vendor —suppliesTo→ Customer-segment` edges. `suppliesTo` is `fan≥3` (one vendor supplies many segments; raw cosine prec@1 is a dismal 0.06). The gate proposes top-5 candidate segments, hands them to a Flash-Lite ratify pass at ~$8/M edges, accepts the ratified pick at precision 0.81, and links. Across the 1M graph this relation alone fires ~120M times; ratifying instead of minting saves the full composition cost on ~97M of them.
**Economic mechanism.** Fuzzy regime → top-k propose → cheap-LLM ratify, the *middle* of the three bands. $8/M is two orders of magnitude under a full compose-and-mint.
**Demand on the interface.** The Provider needs a `findOrCreate` decision method whose return distinguishes `link | mint | quarantine`, and the `fan≥3` case must route to a ratify port (`adjudicate`/`adjudicationAvailable`) — `create()`/`upsert()` cannot express "propose-then-ratify." The decision return must surface `mechanism` (rank vs ratify) so cost can be attributed per relation.

### 3. `frozen` stratum as the dedup identity key
**Use case.** Every generated `Company` Thing has `legalName: Frozen()` and `domain: Frozen()`. When startup #800,121's cascade emits a Company whose frozen `domain` already exists, COLLAPSE matches on the frozen key at cos≈0.99 and links rather than minting a second `Stripe`. The frozen fields are *never regenerated* on any subsequent pass — they are the entity's identity, computed once.
**Economic mechanism.** `frozen` = the COLLAPSE dedup key in symmetric-centered mode. Freezing identity-bearing fields means the dedup signature is stable across passes; an entity matched once stays matched forever, so re-runs cost zero re-composition for it.
**Demand on the interface.** `TokenStratum.frozen` must be **settable-once and identity-bearing** — the Provider must reject mutation of a frozen field and must expose `stratumOf(noun, field)` so the COLLAPSE matcher can assemble its dedup signature from exactly the frozen fields. Frozen ≠ merely immutable; it is the declared identity surface.

### 4. `composition` stratum as a render-time multi-armed bandit
**Use case.** A landing-page cascade emits `headline: Composition({variants:[h1,h2,h3,h4,h5]})`. The five variants are minted *once* (one LLM call producing five) and thereafter the field is never regenerated — `pickComposition(thingRef,'headline',idx)` selects an arm at render-time, and a bandit reallocates traffic toward the winning arm by conversion. 1M startups × 8 composition fields = 8M fields that each cost one batched compose and then *zero* ongoing generation while still A/B-improving.
**Economic mechanism.** `composition` converts "regenerate copy to optimize" (recurring LLM cost) into "pick among frozen variants" (zero LLM cost, pure bandit arithmetic). Optimization happens in arm-selection space, not generation space.
**Demand on the interface.** `composition` fields must be **mint-once, mutate-only-via-`pickComposition`**, with `variants` required and direct assignment rejected. The Provider needs `compositionFields(noun)` so a bandit runtime can enumerate every optimizable arm across the graph without re-reading prompts. `pickComposition` must be idempotent-safe and cheap (a pointer move, not a write of new content).

### 5. `negotiable` fill-once defers cost to the stage that can pay it
**Use case.** A startup's `Pricing.tier` is `Negotiable()` — generated null at idea stage because pricing can't be known yet. 200,000 of those startups never reach the monetization stage; their `negotiable` pricing fields are *never filled*, costing zero. The 30,000 that do reach it fill the slot exactly once, downstream, when the ICP and cost model exist.
**Economic mechanism.** `negotiable` is lazy-compute: cost is incurred only by the survivors that reach the filling stage, not paid eagerly across all candidates. It is the economic dual of `frozen` — frozen is "pay once up front, never again"; negotiable is "pay once later, only if you survive."
**Demand on the interface.** `negotiable` must be **fill-once**: the Provider permits exactly one transition from null → value and then locks. A second fill must be rejected (it would mean a later stage is redoing a settled decision). `stratumOf` must distinguish negotiable from expression so the cascade scheduler knows which fields are *expected* null vs missing.

### 6. Escalation: uncertainty quarantines, never auto-mints
**Use case.** Startup #55,030's cascade tries to link `partneredWith → Company` for "Atlas." The functional rank returns two near-tied candidates (Atlas the fintech, Atlas the HR platform) at 0.86/0.85 — inside the ESCALATE band, below the 0.92 accept floor. The gate returns `{kind:'quarantine'}`. The edge is parked, a stronger model (or human) adjudicates out-of-band, and the cascade either continues with a placeholder or routes to review.
**Economic mechanism.** ESCALATE is the third branch that prevents the two catastrophic outcomes: a *wrong collapse* (two companies merged into one, corrupting the commons for all startups that touch it) and an *eager mint on uncertainty* (a duplicate that pollutes the vocabulary). Quarantine spends a little (stronger model) to protect the integrity of millions of downstream links.
**Demand on the interface.** `Action` needs an **`escalate`/quarantined status distinct from `failed`** — a quarantined edge is not an error, it is a deferred decision awaiting adjudication. The current `ActionStatus` (pending/active/completed/failed/cancelled) lacks this; a quarantined Action must be re-drivable when adjudication arrives, which `failed` semantics do not support.

### 7. The asymmetric/symmetric mode is selected by call-site shape, never globally
**Use case.** Within one cascade tick, two calls fire: `field:'~>Occupation'` resolving "the person who closes deals" → wants **asymmetric MATCH** (query-prefix the role description, match against bare O*NET docs; cos≈0.9 to `Sales Representative`). Immediately after, `$.Occupation.findOrCreate("Account Executive")` for *dedup* → wants **symmetric-centered COLLAPSE** (is this the same node as an existing one?; mean-centered cos≈0.94). If the modes were flipped, the MATCH would compress to cos≈0.07 and the link would fail, forcing a wrong mint.
**Economic mechanism.** Relationship-resolution (name→other-type) = MATCH/asymmetric; entity-dedup (same-type) = COLLAPSE/symmetric. Picking the right mode per call is literally the difference between cos≈0.9 (correct link) and cos≈0.07 (catastrophic miss → spurious mint).
**Demand on the interface.** **Embeddings mode must be selectable per-call and inferred from call-site shape** (`~>` field-resolution ⇒ MATCH; `Noun.findOrCreate` ⇒ COLLAPSE), and the Provider must never expose a global mode flag a developer can flip. The shape *is* the mode selector; the developer never names the mode.

### 8. Cross-startup entity reuse — the commons of canonical Things
**Use case.** `Stripe`, `Postgres`, `Founder` (archetype), `Seed` (stage), `B2B SaaS` (model) are each *one* Thing in the graph, pointed at by hundreds of thousands of startups via `usesPaymentProvider →`, `builtOn →`, `foundedBy →`, `atStage →`, `hasModel →`. Startup #999,999 links all five in microseconds for fractions of a cent because every one already exists and is COLLAPSE-canonical.
**Economic mechanism.** The graph is a deduplicated commons; high-fan canonical entities are minted once across the entire corpus and linked forever after. The reuse rate climbs monotonically with corpus size — the more startups, the higher the link:mint ratio.
**Demand on the interface.** `Thing` identity must be **stable and shareable across tenants/startups** — a Thing minted by one cascade must be `link`-able by another, which means the dedup key (frozen fields) must be content-derived and tenant-independent. The Provider's `find`/`search` must surface the canonical Thing regardless of which startup originally minted it.

### 9. ICP emergence from COLLAPSE clusters
**Use case.** 60,000 startups independently generate problem statements that COLLAPSE-cluster (symmetric-centered) around "finance teams can't reconcile multi-currency invoices fast enough." That cluster *is* an emergent ICP — discovered, not designed, by watching which Problem Things accrete the most inbound `solves <-` edges. The cascade for startup #60,001 in that space links to the canonical Problem and inherits the cluster's accumulated enrichment for free.
**Economic mechanism.** COLLAPSE clustering turns dedup into market intelligence: the densest collapse-clusters reveal where demand concentrates, and entities in those clusters carry pre-computed enrichment that new startups inherit by linking. Dedup pays a *second dividend* as ICP discovery.
**Demand on the interface.** COLLAPSE must produce a **canonical Thing that accumulates provenance** (which startups/ICPs collapsed into it) rather than discarding the duplicates. `findOrCreateMany` returning per-item `{decision, thing, confidence}` lets a cohort runtime measure cluster density. The collapse must preserve "≥2 sources linked to one canonical" (provenance kept, not two rows).

### 10. Online-FIND vs offline-COLLAPSE as a per-Noun economic policy
**Use case.** `Problem` and cross-standard `Verb` Nouns are high-overlap (98% of generated problems duplicate an existing one) — they use **online FIND** in the pre-gen router, so the cascade never even composes the duplicate; zero post-hoc cleanup. `LandingPageCopy` is low-overlap (almost always novel) — it uses **offline COLLAPSE** in a batch sweep, so generation stays fast and the rare duplicate is reconciled later.
**Economic mechanism.** Per-Noun timing policy optimizes the cost-of-generation vs cost-of-cleanup tradeoff. High-overlap Nouns pay a small per-edge FIND tax to avoid composing duplicates at all; low-overlap Nouns skip the tax and accept a cheap batch sweep.
**Demand on the interface.** The Provider must let **FIND-timing be configured per-Noun** (online gate vs offline batch COLLAPSE), and `TokenStratum`/Noun metadata must declare the expected overlap regime. `findOrCreate` (online) and a batch collapse pass (offline) must share one decision core so the *meaning* of a match is identical regardless of timing.

### 11. The 1000× moment — the nearly-free millionth startup
**Use case.** Startup #1,000,000 ("a vertical SaaS for dental-practice scheduling") cascades through 600 edges. 580 of them link to existing canonical Things (Industry: Healthcare IT; Occupation: Dental Office Manager; Tech: React+Postgres+Stripe; Problem: no-show reduction; Model: B2B SaaS). Only 20 edges introduce genuinely novel tails and pay LLM composition. Total marginal cost: 580 embedding-rank queries (~free) + 20 composes. The startup that would have cost \$X to generate from scratch costs ~3% of X.
**Economic mechanism.** This is the entire thesis realized: at corpus saturation the link:mint ratio is ~29:1, and the marginal startup costs only its novel composition. The gate is what converts corpus size into marginal-cost collapse.
**Demand on the interface.** The interface must make **link-vs-mint cheap to *count* per startup** — `findOrCreateMany` must return a verdict list whose `decision` field aggregates into a "novelty budget" (how many mints this startup actually required). The gate must be the *only* path to relation creation so no edge bypasses the economic count (no raw `perform`/`create` that skips findOrCreate for relation-type Actions).

### 12. Bandit composition over `composition` strata across the corpus
**Use case.** A global bandit watches every `Composition()` field across all 1M startups. It discovers that for fintech-vertical landing pages, headline-variant-3 patterns ("Stop losing $X to Y") convert 18% better, and biases `pickComposition` toward that arm for new fintech startups *without regenerating a single headline*. The optimization is corpus-wide arm-selection, paid in arithmetic, not tokens.
**Economic mechanism.** `composition` strata aggregate into a corpus-scale bandit: the search for "what works" happens over a fixed variant space, so improvement is decoupled from generation cost. Optimization scales with traffic, not with LLM spend.
**Demand on the interface.** `compositionFields(noun)` must enumerate variants across the whole corpus uniformly so a bandit can pool arm-statistics across startups, and `pickComposition` must record the chosen arm on the Action (or Thing) so reward attribution (conversion → arm) is auditable. Variants must be addressable by stable index so cross-startup arm-pooling is well-defined.

## Highest-leverage use case + sharpest DEMAND

**Highest leverage: #1 / #2 together — every relation edge routed through the fan-typed FIND|CREATE|ESCALATE gate, with regime read off the `Frame`.** I hold this position over the strata cases and the 1000× moment because the strata are *optimizations on already-decided entities*, and the 1000× moment is the *consequence*, not the *mechanism*. The mechanism that produces the 1000× moment is the per-edge decision, fired billions of times, choosing functional-rank vs fuzzy-ratify based on the relation's `fan`. If that single decision is right, the marginal-cost collapse follows automatically; if it is wrong (one global cosine floor, no regime split), the graph either fractures into near-duplicates (no reuse, no savings) or wrong-collapses (corrupted commons). Everything else in this lens is downstream of getting *this one edge decision* economically correct, billions of times, cheaply.

**Sharpest DEMAND:** **`Frame` must carry, per-relation, the `fan` and `direction` the gate reads to select regime — and the Provider's relation-creation path must route *exclusively* through a `findOrCreate`-style decision method returning `link | mint | quarantine`, never a bare `create`/`upsert` for relation-type Actions.** The Frame is where regime selection must live (the gate must not infer fan from runtime data), and the decision method's three-way return is the only shape that expresses the three economic branches.

## Requirements this lens imposes on the committed interface

- **`Frame` carries per-relation `fan` (numeric/banded) and `direction` (`direction-bearing | similarity-aligned`)** so the gate reads functional-vs-fuzzy regime *off the Verb's frame*, not from runtime sampling. `PredicateSpec.{rel,dstType,fan,direction,closedPool?}` must be derivable from a Verb's `Frame`.
- **The Provider needs a `findOrCreate` decision method returning `link | mint | quarantine`** (not just `create`) — three branches, with `mechanism` (rank vs ratify) and `confidence` surfaced for cost attribution. `findOrCreateMany` returns a per-item verdict list that aggregates into a per-startup novelty budget.
- **Relation-type Actions must be mintable *only* through the gate** — no raw `perform`/`create` path that bypasses the economic count, or the link:mint accounting (the whole savings story) leaks.
- **`Action` needs a `quarantined`/`escalate` status distinct from `failed`** — re-drivable when adjudication arrives; quarantine is a deferred decision, not an error.
- **`TokenStratum.frozen` must be settable-once and identity-bearing** — the Provider rejects mutation and exposes `stratumOf` so the COLLAPSE matcher assembles its dedup signature from exactly the frozen fields. Frozen ≠ merely immutable; it is the declared identity surface, and the dedup key must be content-derived and tenant-independent so Things are shareable across startups.
- **`TokenStratum.negotiable` must be fill-once** — exactly one null→value transition, then locked; the scheduler distinguishes "expected null" from "missing."
- **`TokenStratum.composition` must be mint-once, mutate-only-via-`pickComposition`** with `variants` required, direct assignment rejected, stable index addressing, and `compositionFields(noun)` enumerable corpus-wide so a bandit pools arm-statistics across startups. `pickComposition` is a cheap pointer move and records the chosen arm for reward attribution.
- **Embeddings mode is selectable per-call and inferred from call-site shape** (`~>` field-resolution ⇒ asymmetric MATCH; `Noun.findOrCreate` ⇒ symmetric-centered COLLAPSE) — never a global flag the developer can flip. The shape is the mode selector.
- **COLLAPSE must produce a canonical Thing that accumulates provenance** (≥2 sources → one canonical, duplicates linked not discarded) so dedup doubles as ICP-cluster intelligence.
- **FIND-timing must be a per-Noun policy** (online gate vs offline batch COLLAPSE) sharing one decision core, with Noun metadata declaring the expected-overlap regime.
- **Uncertainty must fail toward escalate/create, never auto-mint or auto-collapse** — the gate's default on ambiguity is quarantine, protecting the integrity of the shared commons across millions of downstream links.
